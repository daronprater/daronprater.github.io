Understanding Sleep Apnea

Sleep apnea is a common sleep abnormality where pauses in breathing, which can last up to 10 seconds, occur due to narrowing of the upper airway. This causes a lack of air to enter the lungs resulting in less oxygen reaching vital organs. The body often tries to compensate to overcome this obstruction by putting forth more effort to breathe air through a narrower airway, which causes snoring.
